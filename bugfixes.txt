================================================================================
2026-04-15 (round 2)
================================================================================

[engine.py / pipeline.py] Newton step N_total used buffer size instead of total samples seen
  _influence_newton divides the H^{-1}·grad step by N_total, which was passed as
  len(buf_y) (~600, the replay buffer size). The correct N is the total number of
  samples the model has trained on, which grows to ~16000 by batch 80. Using 600
  instead made every Newton step ~26x too large, causing systematic accuracy
  collapse from 0.90 to 0.69 and 16/29 utility-drop rollbacks.
  Fix: track n_seen in the pipeline and pass it as n_total to unlearn(); fall back
  to len(y_train) if not provided.

[engine.py] No magnitude cap on Newton step allowed arbitrarily large weight updates
  Even with correct N_total, a poorly conditioned Hessian can produce a step that
  is large in L2 norm, destabilising the model on the first few batches when N is
  still small.
  Fix: clip the step vector to L2 norm <= 0.1 before applying it.

[preservation.py] EWC recovery used too few steps to undo oversized Newton updates
  check_and_recover ran only 10 EWC gradient steps, which was insufficient to
  recover accuracy when the Newton step had perturbed weights significantly.
  Fix: increase recovery steps from 10 to 25.

================================================================================
2026-04-15 (round 6)
================================================================================

[engine.py] LiSSA HVP in _influence_newton used only candidate samples (15) not full training data
  The Newton step's LiSSA loop estimated the Hessian-vector product using X_cand
  (15 candidate samples), not the full replay buffer (~600 samples). With only 15
  samples the HVP was extremely noisy — the resulting direction was sometimes right
  (batch 35: SPD 0.2391→0.2132) but usually garbage (batches 49, 56, 63: identical
  before/after). The InfluenceEstimator._lissa() correctly uses buf_X/buf_y for HVP;
  _influence_newton was inconsistent with this.
  Fix: add X_ref/y_ref parameters to _influence_newton and pass X_train/y_train
  (the full replay buffer) for HVP. X_cand/y_cand are still used only for grad_sum.



[engine.py] Fixed Newton step clip caused by constant max_norm (too small or too large)
  clip=0.05 was too small to flip enough window predictions to visibly change SPD
  (many events showed SPD before = SPD after to 4 decimal places). clip=0.10 (from
  round 3) was too large and caused accuracy collapse. A constant clip cannot be
  right for all stages of the run: when SPD=0.30, an aggressive step is justified;
  when SPD=0.12 (near threshold), a tiny step is needed to avoid overshooting.
  Fix: make max_step_norm adaptive — computed as clip(0.5 * spd_excess, 0.02, 0.10)
  where spd_excess = max(0, snapshot.spd - threshold). Pass from pipeline to
  unlearn() and down to _influence_newton() via a new max_step_norm parameter.

[pipeline.py] Fine-tuning 5 steps washing out fairness gain
  5 steps of fine-tuning on the biased replay buffer re-introduced approximately
  as much bias as the Newton step removed, leaving many events with zero net SPD
  change. Reduced to 2 steps: enough for partial accuracy recovery without
  completely cancelling the Newton step's fairness correction.

[compas_demo.py] consecutive_violations too low (5) triggered too many events
  With structural COMPAS bias, SPD exceeds the threshold almost every batch.
  consecutive_violations=5 produced 17-19 unlearning events, causing cumulative
  accuracy drift even when individual drops were within tolerance.
  Fix: raise to 7 to reduce event frequency.

[compas_demo.py] accuracy_drop_tolerance too tight at 0.04
  With adaptive (smaller) steps near the threshold, the 0.04 tolerance rejected
  events that were making genuine small improvements.
  Fix: raise to 0.06 to match the wider range of step sizes now in use.



[pipeline.py] Cumulative accuracy drift from compounding per-event tolerance
  Each accepted unlearning event was allowed to drop accuracy by up to 0.08.
  With 13 accepted events, this compounds: 0.88 * (1-0.05)^13 ≈ 0.50, matching
  the observed collapse to 0.51 at batch 76. The utility check only prevented
  single-event drops, not cumulative drift across the run.
  Fix: after every accepted unlearning, immediately call preserver.fine_tune()
  for 5 steps on the replay buffer to recover accuracy before the next batch.

[engine.py] Newton step clip too large, causing excessive per-event accuracy cost
  The L2-norm clip at 0.1 allowed steps large enough that each event traded
  significant accuracy for fairness gain. Over many events this compounded.
  Fix: tighten clip from 0.1 to 0.05, halving the per-event weight perturbation.

[compas_demo.py] accuracy_drop_tolerance too loose at 0.08
  An 8% per-event accuracy drop acceptance threshold is too permissive when
  many unlearning events fire, since small drops compound across the run.
  Fix: tighten to 0.04 to reject events where the Newton step costs too much
  accuracy even before fine-tuning recovery.



[engine.py] 1/N_total scaling made Newton steps negligibly small (opposite problem)
  Using n_seen (~16000) as N_total made the step v/16000 ≈ 0.000006 — far below
  the 0.1 clip, so the clip never fired and SPD before/after were identical to 4
  decimal places on most events (e.g. 0.2485->0.2485). The prior fix traded
  accuracy collapse for a completely inert unlearner.
  Root cause: 1/N scaling is mathematically correct for influence functions but
  practically useless for fairness unlearning — removing 15/16000 points (~0.1%
  of training data) has a negligible parameter effect by design.
  Fix: remove the 1/N_total division entirely. The L2-norm clip at 0.1 is the
  right and only guard against accuracy collapse. Also removed n_total parameter
  plumbing from unlearn() and pipeline since it is no longer used.



[pipeline.py] Fairness check after unlearning used a bare single-batch SPD
  Post-unlearning `new_snapshot` was computed from a fresh FairnessMonitor fed
  only the current 200-sample batch. SPD over 200 samples is high-variance,
  causing good unlearning events to be falsely rejected as "fairness worsened".
  Fix: copy the existing rolling window history into temp_monitor and only
  replace the most-recent batch slice with fresh predictions, making the
  comparison apples-to-apples with `snapshot`. Tolerance tightened 0.05 -> 0.02.

[engine.py] LiSSA iteration diverging in _influence_newton
  Update rule `v = grad_sum + v - hvp` with `hvp = (H + λI)·v` expands to
  `v = grad_sum + (I - H - λI)·v`. Convergence requires spectral radius of
  (I - H_d) < 1, which fails for large Hessian eigenvalues. Without normalising
  grad_sum first, v blew up and produced wild Newton steps (e.g. SPD 0.29->0.40).
  Fix: normalise grad_sum before the iteration and undo the scale afterwards;
  increased iterations from 5 to 10.

[pipeline.py] Model skipped training on accepted-unlearning batches
  After a successful unlearning + detector.reset(), the pipeline jumped straight
  to replay_buffer.add(batch) without calling model.update(batch.X, batch.y).
  Over 15 accepted events that silently dropped ~3000 training samples.
  Fix: call self.model.update(batch.X, batch.y) after accepting unlearning.

[pipeline.py] Retain set overlapped with forget candidates in unlearning
  rep_X/rep_y for the gradient reversal retain signal was sampled from the whole
  replay buffer, including the candidate indices being unlearned. The "forget"
  gradient was being immediately counteracted by the "retain" gradient on the
  same points.
  Fix: build retain_mask excluding cand_idx before sampling the retain set.
