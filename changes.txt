  ---                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    
  Theoretical contributions needed                                                                                                                                                                                                                       

  This is the hardest and most important gap. Two viable angles:

  Angle 1 — Certified fairness unlearning
  Certified unlearning (Guo et al. 2020, Sekhari et al. 2021) requires the post-unlearning model to be statistically indistinguishable from one retrained without those points. No one has formally unified this with fairness constraints in a streaming
   setting. That's your gap.

  You'd need to prove something like:

  ▎ "After k unlearning events, our method achieves (ε,δ)-certified unlearning while maintaining |SPD| ≤ τ with probability 1−δ under distribution shift"

  Angle 2 — Fairness-accuracy-unlearning tradeoff
  Prove a formal tradeoff: you cannot simultaneously have certified unlearning + fairness guarantee + bounded accuracy loss. Then show your method achieves the Pareto frontier of this tradeoff. This is publishable as a standalone theoretical result.

  Model — yes, you need neural networks

  Logistic regression limits you to venues that accept "convex models only" scope. For top-tier:

  Short term (manageable): Extend to 2-layer MLP using TracIn (Pruthi et al., NeurIPS 2020) for approximate influence functions instead of exact Hessian inversion. TracIn computes influence via gradient checkpoints — works for any differentiable
  model.

  Stronger but harder: Reformulate unlearning as a constrained optimization problem that applies to any model class, then prove convergence bounds.
